BlurShield Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2025

Data we collect

None. BlurShield does not collect, transmit, or store any personal data.

What stays on your device

The list of domains you add and your current blur state are stored locally using Chrome's built-in chrome.storage API. This data never leaves your browser and is never sent to any server.

Permissions used

storage — to save your protected domain list across sessions.

scripting / tabs — to apply the blur overlay to matching pages when screen sharing is detected.

host_permissions (<all_urls>) — required to inject the screen-share detector and blur overlay into any page you've added to your list.

Third parties

BlurShield has no backend, no analytics, and no third-party integrations of any kind.
